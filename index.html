Who Was Ed Gein?

Ed Gein — 1957 arrest mugshot

Edward Theodore Gein — the Butcher of Plainfield — was an American murderer and grave robber from Wisconsin. After his domineering mother died in 1945, his mind unravelled. When police searched his farmhouse in November 1957 they discovered a house of horrors that shocked the world.

The Case for Gein

Body of Work
Dahmer inspired one Netflix series. Gein inspired Psycho, The Texas Chain Saw Massacre, and The Silence of the Lambs — three of the greatest horror films ever made.
Nature of Crime
Gein dug up the dead, wore their skin, upholstered furniture with human remains, and used skulls as bowls. The farmhouse was a sustained, years-long work of nightmare.
Media Bias
Gein was arrested in 1957 — before TV dominated American life. Dahmer's fame is an accident of timing and telegenic appearance.
